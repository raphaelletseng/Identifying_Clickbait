A War on Graffiti in Brazil
São Paulo Djan Ivson has been prosecuted seven times for vandalism. It’s becoming an occupational hazard. The 33-year-old is Brazil’s most vocal practitioner and defender of “pichação,” known as “pixo,” a distinctive form of calligraphic graffiti that covers everything from skyscrapers to burnt-out cars here in South America’s biggest city.
