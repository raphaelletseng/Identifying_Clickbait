The Chainsmokers ' "Closer" kept getting closer to a noteworthy Billboard Hot 100 longevity record. Now, it's tied it. As it holds at No. 10 on the March 25-dated Hot 100, the song, featuring Halsey , spends a 32nd week in the top 10, matching the mark for the most weeks logged in the bracket, dating to the chart's Aug. 4, 1958, inception. "Closer" equals the 32 weeks that LeAnn Rimes ' "How Do I Live" accumulated in the top 10 in 1997-98.  "Closer," which spent 12 weeks at No. 1 on the Hot 100 beginning Sept. 3, has charted all 32 of its weeks so far on the Hot 100 in the top 10 (dating to its Aug. 20 debut at No. 9), also a record for the most frames that a song has spent in the chart's top 10 .
