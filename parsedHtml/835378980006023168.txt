KUALA LUMPUR, Malaysia—The operator of Malaysia’s airports said there is no risk to travelers at Kuala Lumpur International Airport from the deadly nerve agent that was used to kill Kim Jong Nam there last week.

In a statement, Malaysia Airports Holdings Bhd. said late Friday that there have been no irregularities in the reported medical cases at the airport’s clinic since Kim Jong Nam’s half brother was attacked on Feb. 13.
